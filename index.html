Overview

Spatial language Applications

An understanding of spatial language can benefit many areas of research endeavors relating to and/or making use of human language, including robotics, navigation, geographic information systems, traffic management, and query answering systems.

Spatial Language Understanding

Approaches to spatial language understanding have focused on developing formalisms to map natural language expressions to spatial representation appropriate for spatial reasoning [7, 4, 5], or studying spatial cognition in order to improve spatial language understanding by imitating use of such language by humans [8]. Despite all the research efforts toward standardizing the extraction of spatial information from natural language including designing annotation schema, creating linguistic corpora, etc, many research works in the related applications use their own invented datasets and a limited set of application-specific concepts when they need to extract the spa- tial information.

Recent Research Trend

The recent research trend in combining vision and language also emphasizes the relevance of spatial semantics as it relates to the physical world and can be visualized. Applications of text to scene conversion or even generating textual description for images and videos are influenced by the ability of machines for spatial understanding and reasoning. Learning how to map this information onto a formal representation from text is one important research direction that can help the mentioned applications.

Standardizing Spatial Information Extraction

Compared to other linguistic tasks, standardizing a task for spatial information extraction seems to be challenging, particularly, an agreeable set of concepts and relationships and a formal spatial meaning representation that is domain independent is harder to obtain compared to other type of semantics such as temporal semantics. Attempts to arrive at a common set of basic concepts and relationships as well as making existing corpora inter-operable, however, can help avoid duplicated efforts within as well as across fields and instead focus on further developments in the respective fields for learning and reasoning.

Highlight

The theme of this workshop remains generic, we aim to accommodate all the related research that deal with learning and reasoning about spatial information and have the natural language as a component in one way or another or in combination with other modalities.

Topics Summary

Our topics include but not limited to the following research aspects:
  • Spatial meaning representations, ontologies, annotation schemes, linguistic corpora.
  • Spatial information extraction from natural language.
  • Spatial information extraction in robotics, multimodal environments, navigational instructions.
  • Text mining for spatial information in GIS systems.
  • Spatial information in query answering systems, answering locative questions, such as where-questions.
  • Spatial information for visual question answering
  • Quantitative and qualitative reasoning with spatial information
  • Spatial reasoning based on natural language
  • Spatial reasoning based on multimodal information (vision and language)
  • Extraction of spatial common sense knowledge
  • Visualization of spatial language in 2-D and 3-D
  • Spatial natural language generation
  • Spatial language grounding
